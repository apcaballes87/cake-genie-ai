## Content Quality Issues

### Issue 1: /how-to-order — Thin Content (~450 words)
**Problem:** The page describes a 4-step process but contains approximately 450 words — well below the 800-word minimum for a service page. There is no FAQ section, no troubleshooting guide, no example of what happens if the AI misreads an image.
**Risk:** AI platforms will not cite this page; it provides no quotable information beyond the site itself.
**Fix:** Expand to 800-1,200 words. Add: "What if the AI estimate seems too high or low?", "Can I order without an image?", "What happens after I place my order?", and at least one real order example with a result.

### Issue 2: All Blog Posts — Organization-as-Author
**Problem:** `"author":{"@type":"Organization","name":"Genie.ph"}` on all blog posts. This eliminates Person-level E-E-A-T signals. Google's December 2025 QRG update explicitly extends expertise evaluation to all competitive queries.
**Fix:** Add a named author (Alan Paris Caballes or a designated content lead) with a Person schema that includes `sameAs` links to LinkedIn/Twitter and a brief bio. Create an `/author/alan-paris-caballes` page with credentials and photo.

### Issue 3: Jollibee vs McDonald's — No Verification of Pricing Data
**Problem:** Package pricing is presented as definitive (e.g., "Package A: ₱6,500") with no source, no date of verification, and no branch-specific confirmation. If prices have changed, this creates an accuracy liability.
**Fix:** Add a sentence like: *"Prices verified via direct branch inquiry in [Month Year]. Rates vary by location and are subject to change — confirm with your chosen branch before booking."* Add `dateModified` prominently on-page (not just in schema).

### Issue 4: Homepage Reviews — Unverified Source
**Problem:** "4.8/5 based on 40 reviews" has no link to the source platform. This is a trustworthiness red flag for both AI platforms and users.
**Fix:** Link to the Google Maps listing or Facebook Reviews page. If reviews are platform-native (Genie.ph internal), disclose that clearly.

### Issue 5: FAQ — Answer Depth Varies Significantly
**Problem:** "What payment methods do you accept?" (~25 words) is too brief. "What is your refund policy?" (~65 words) is adequate but still vague on timelines. AI platforms extract FAQ answers as direct responses; one-line answers will not be cited.
**Fix:** Expand all FAQ answers to a minimum of 60-80 words. Add specifics: exact payment method names (Maya, GCash, bank transfer, credit card), processing timelines for refunds, and step-by-step escalation paths for issues.

*Impact: High | Effort: Medium | E-E-A-T Dimension: Expertise + Authoritativeness*

Replace `"author":{"@type":"Organization","name":"Genie.ph"}` in all blog post schema with:
```json
"author": {
  "@type": "Person",
  "name": "Alan Paris Caballes",
  "url": "https://genie.ph/author/alan-paris-caballes",
  "sameAs": ["https://linkedin.com/in/[profile]"]
}
```
Create an `/author/alan-paris-caballes` page with: headshot, title (Founder, Genie.ph), the Startup Innovation Summit award, location (Cebu City), and a 2-3 sentence bio explaining his background in tech and the local cake market. Display this byline visibly on every blog post above the fold.

**Why this matters:** Per the December 2025 QRG update, E-E-A-T signals now apply to ALL competitive queries. A named expert author with a credentialed profile page is the single highest-leverage E-E-A-T action for a content-heavy site.

**Recommendation 2: Add a "Content Accuracy" Disclosure to All Data-Driven Blog Posts**
*Impact: High | Effort: Low | E-E-A-T Dimension: Trustworthiness*

Every article containing pricing (Jollibee vs McDonald's, custom cake guide) should display a visible notice:
> *"Prices and package details were verified in [Month Year]. Please confirm current rates directly with the relevant venue or baker, as these may change."*

Additionally, add `dateModified` visibly on-page (not just in schema) for all blog posts. This is a 30-minute implementation task with significant trust impact.

**Recommendation 3: Add Real Customer Stories to the About Page and FAQ**
*Impact: High | Effort: Medium | E-E-A-T Dimension: Experience*

The site has 40 verified customer reviews — these are an untapped asset. Select 5-8 reviews with:
- Customer first name and initial (e.g., "Maria T., Cebu City")
- The specific occasion (daughter's 7th birthday)
- A specific outcome ("The cake looked exactly like the photo I uploaded")
- Star rating with source link (Google Maps or Facebook)

Embed these on /about and create a dedicated `/reviews` or `/testimonials` page. Link the review badge on the homepage to this page. This directly addresses the weakest E-E-A-T dimension (Experience: 10/25) without requiring new content creation.

**Recommendation 4: Expand /how-to-order to 900+ Words with Real Examples**
*Impact: Medium | Effort: Medium | E-E-A-T Dimension: Experience + Expertise*

The current ~450-word page is below the threshold for AI citability. Add:

**Recommendation 5: Link the 4.8/5 Rating to a Verifiable Source**
*Impact: Medium | Effort: Low | E-E-A-T Dimension: Trustworthiness*

- If reviews are on Google Business Profile: link the badge to the Google Maps listing
